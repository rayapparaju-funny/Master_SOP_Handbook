🏬 Hub Portal Activities

📥 Inbound Process

1.1 Receiving Manifest from Master Manifest

This process involves receiving manifests that arrive at the hub via Master Manifest (MM). The Hub Incharge must verify and accept incoming shipments.

Step 1: Navigate to Hub Portal and click on "MM Reaching Hub".

Step 2: Click on "Receive on Trip".

Step 3: Please scan the vehicle seal number, then scan all the manifests on the right side, click Submit, and obtain the POD.

Step 4: In case the trip is invalid, click on "Without Vehicle", then scan all the manifests, click "Unloading Complete", and obtain the POD.

Step 5: Click on "Receive".

Step 6: From the dropdown, select Transporter and scan all the manifests.

Step 7: Click on "Submit & Print" to generate and print the POD (Proof Of Delivery).

🗺️ Process Flow:

MM Reaching Hub
→
Receive on Trip
→
Scan Seal & Manifests
→
Submit & Get POD

1.2 Receiving Shipments from Manifests

After receiving the manifest, scan individual AWBs to receive shipments into the system.

Step 1: Click on "Receive Manifests" in the portal.

Step 2: Scan the seal number on each manifest and scan all AWB numbers to receive the shipments in the manifests. The number of scanned/unscanned AWBs will reflect.

🗺️ Process Flow:

Receive Manifests
→
Scan Manifest Seal
→
Scan AWBs
→
Verify Count

1.3 Sorting

Sort shipments based on pincode, route, type, and criticality for smooth delivery operations.

Step 1: Scan shipment and sort pincode-wise in bins.

Step 2: Sort all Fresh D-1, NA, and NC shipments route-wise.

Step 3: While closing the runsheet, sort all undelivered shipments based on their final status.

Step 4: Scan and sort all RTS and Volumetric shipments into appropriate bins.

🗺️ Process Flow:

→
Sort Pincode-wise

📞 Unable to Contact Customer (IVRS)

Steps to handle cases when the customer cannot be contacted via call masking.

This process ensures proper handling of customer unreachability using IVR-assisted call flow and automated system updates.

Step 1: Click on Unable to Scan in the "My Runsheets" section.

Step 2: Click on Unable to Contact Customer.

Step 3: If no call history is found, click on Call Customer to initiate a call manually.

Step 4: If the customer is not contactable, click Yes to enable the automated IVR calling system.

Step 5: The call will automatically disconnect and a pop-up will show the IVR response of the customer.

Step 6: The rider should act as per the IVR response. If the customer confirms, click on Attempt Delivery.

Step 7: The rider will be redirected to the order sheet. Click Scan AWB to mark status and continue with delivery flow.

Step 8: If the customer remains unreachable, a pop-up will appear, and the status will be marked as NC (Not Contactable). Click Close to finish.

📊 Dashboard & Reports

🔴 Live Dashboard

Real-time Hub & Shipment Monitoring

Live: Data is updated immediately or continuously, showing the current status.
Dashboard: A single screen that organizes multiple visual elements (charts, tables, maps) for quick understanding.

Step 1: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → GEM – Delivery Champion
Tracks hub performance, rider ranking, and delivery efficiency. Highlights top riders based on DSR, FASR/FPSR, and productivity. Helps identify best and low performers for recognition or improvement.

Step 2: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → Pendency
Tracks pending shipments (Critical, Forward, Reverse) not attempted, delivered, or connected to DC. Shows client/pincode-wise breakup with ageing buckets (0, 1, 2–4, 5–6, 6+). Includes open escalated orders.

Step 3: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → Performance
Displays forward/reverse metrics — Attempt%, FASR/FPSR, DSR, and runsheet summary. Shows rider/pincode-wise performance, fake/inactive runsheets, and success rates.

Step 4: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → Live NC Dashboard
Shows real-time NC (Not Connected/Contactable) shipments. Used to monitor pending cases and assign riders for follow-up and closure.

Step 5: Click on "Receive on Trip" → Start receiving shipments → Fake Tracker Dashboard
Monitors fake cases confirmed via IVR. Provides rider-wise and AWB-wise visibility, helping detect and control fake delivery attempts.

Step 6: Scan vehicle seal number → Scan all manifests → Click Submit → Obtain POD → FM / RTS Report
Shows Forward Movement and Return to Sender performance. Covers runsheet summary, strike rate, and RTS shipment status (connected, pending, or in transit).

Step 7: Click on "Without Vehicle" → Scan manifests → Click “Unloading Complete” → Obtain POD → NC Calling Sheet
Daily tracker for NC follow-ups. Records customer responses, reasons, and actions taken to resolve NC shipments within TAT.

Step 8: Click on "Receive" → Upload image → SDD Arrival
Monitors Same Day Delivery shipments for on-time arrival and delivery. Tracks cut-off time, scan-in/out, and rider assignment.

Step 9: Select Transporter → Scan all manifests → Shipment Tally
Reconciles physical vs. system shipment counts. Identifies short, excess, or unscanned shipments to maintain hub inventory accuracy.

Step 10: Click on "Submit & Print" → Generate and print POD → Trends
Provides visual trends of shipment clearance and operational hygiene. Shows daily, weekly, and monthly data on pendency, performance, and fake cases to ensure process adherence.

📈 HI Dashboard

Key Performance Indicators

HI Dashboard: Used by the Hub Incharge to monitor hub activities, rider performance, deliveries, COD collections, and other operational KPIs.

Step 1: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → Rider Hiring
Total Required Rider = riders needed; LMA Leads = Last Mile Aggregator leads; Current DAU = active riders; Deficit = hiring gap; Winback Riders = riders inactive > 8 days.

Step 2: Click on "Receive on Trip" → Start receiving shipments → Customer Service
View forward/reverse metrics: FASR, FPSR, DSR, Fake Runsheets, CCI Resolution, Address Issues, RTO% (W-4), ZA_RTO% (W-4). Includes rider-wise/pincode-wise performance and runsheet summary for actions.

Step 3: Scan vehicle seal → Scan manifests → Submit → Obtain POD → Loss Control
COD Balance = Opening + Collected – Auto_UPI – Bank Deposits – Adjustment; Total Short Deposit = COD pending deposit; Seller Escalation (W-3)%, QC Loss Accepted (W-3)%, and Seller Escalated Debit (W-3) track loss acceptance and amounts.

Step 4: Click on "Without Vehicle" → Scan manifests → “Unloading Complete” → Obtain POD → Landing Prediction
Shows 7-day forecast for FWD, RVP, and Large landings with ±5–10% variation.

Step 5: Click on "Receive" → Upload image → Speed
4hr Attempt% (Target >45%), D0 Attempt% (Target >92%), D1 Attempt% (Target >98%), 24hr/48hr Pickup, and Broadcast Adherence (pickup directly assigned by riders via app).

Step 6: Select Transporter → Scan all manifests → LM Stationary
Check current hub stock and consumption of last-mile stationary.

Step 7: Click "Submit & Print" → Generate POD → Fake Status
Train riders: record correct statuses, avoid 0% hub-portal updates, don’t self-disconnect calls, encourage blind attempts, and communicate consequences for repeated fake callouts. Track the Fake Tracker Dashboard and resolve detected fake cases within 24 hours.

Step 8: Click "Submit & Print" → Generate POD → Trends
Graphs: FASR/FPSR (last 14 days), DoD Fake Runsheet Trend (last 14 days), DoD Fake%, D0/D1 Performance Trend (last 14 days), Address Issues — to monitor operational hygiene and consistency.

📊 Shipment Tally

Daily Shipment Reconciliation

Shipment Tally: The process of counting and verifying incoming shipments against the manifest to ensure accuracy in delivery, inventory, and system records.

Step 1: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → Select Shipment Tally.
Scan the pending shipments under Scan Shipment AWB to begin the reconciliation process.

Step 2: Once the AWB is scanned in Shipment Tally, the system will display the current status of the shipment along with its attempt details.

Step 3: After all pending shipments are scanned, the dashboard will display "All Done", confirming that reconciliation is complete.

🗺️ Process Flow:

Shipments Arrive at Hub/Warehouse
→
Check Against Manifest/AWB
→
Count Total Packages
→
Record Tally in System
→
Verify Accuracy & Confirm

⚠️ Exception Management

Handling Delivery & Operational Exceptions

Exception Management: The process of identifying, analyzing, and resolving issues or deviations that occur during the delivery or logistics process to ensure smooth hub operations.

Step 1: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal → Click on Exception Shipment.

Step 2: From the dropdown menu, select the appropriate reason for the exception.

Step 3: Select Hazy / Label Reprint Shipment for hazy shipments and inscan the shipment.

Step 4: Enter the AWB number twice to confirm and click on Submit.

Step 5: Click on Unable to Identify AWB Number for shipments that cannot be scanned.

Step 6: Scan the Flyer Code available on the shipment. If unavailable, click on Did not find packet ID.

Step 7: A QR code will be displayed on the screen. Use your mobile device to scan the code.

Step 8: Update all relevant details as displayed on the screen and click on Submit.

Step 9: Once submitted, your remarks will be displayed under Request Submitted for tracking and closure.

🗺️ Process Flow:

Identify Exception
→
Analyze Issue
→
Assign to Responsible Team
→
Take Corrective Action
→
Verify Resolution
→
Close Exception

🖥 Valinor Login Portal

🔐 Access & Login

Valinor Portal Access

Valinor Portal: Steps a user follows to securely log in to the Valinor portal, complete KYC details, and access linked systems like the Ecom and Support Portals.

Step 1: Click on "MM Reaching Hub" → Navigate to Hub Portal.
Enter your contact number and click on Send OTP.

Step 2: An OTP will be sent to your registered mobile number. Enter the OTP received.

Step 3: Click on Submit to verify your credentials and log in.

Step 4: After login, you will be redirected to the Ecom Portal and the Support Portal.

Step 5: For KYC completion, click on KYC Pending after logging into Valinor.

Step 6: Click on Update Personal Info to review or modify your details.

Step 7: A new page will open. Update all mandatory details and click Update to save your information.

Step 8: To log out, click the three-dot icon (⋮) and select Logout.
Logging out of Valinor will automatically log you out of all connected portals.

→

💼 Support Portal

Support Portal Access

Support Portal: The portal used to manage and track rider information, payouts, orders, availability, and deductions efficiently.

Step 1: Click on "Support Portal" and navigate to the Support Portal home page to begin rider management.

Step 2: Enter the contact number or Rider ID, then click Submit to fetch rider details.

Step 3: The rider details (name, ID, zone, and status) will be displayed on the screen.

Step 4: Click on Payout to view the rider’s payment summary, including total earnings and pending payouts.

Step 5: Click on Availability to check the rider’s current working status and assigned regions.

Step 6: Under Availability, enter the Rider ID and click Submit to check for prime orders and assignments.

Step 7: Click on Deduction to view details of any deductions or penalties applied to the rider.

Step 8: Click on Orders to review the rider’s active and completed deliveries.

Step 9: Click on Refers to view the total number of referrals made by the rider and their referral status.

🗺️ Process Flow:

Access Support Portal
→
Enter Rider ID / Contact
→
View Rider Details
→
Access Functions (Payout / Availability / Orders / Deductions / Refers)
